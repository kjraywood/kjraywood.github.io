1. Responses to One-Minor Opening

1.1. Natural jump-shift in other-minor

1♦︎ – 3♣︎ = natural, inv.
 • Needed because 1♦︎–2♣︎ is GF

1♣︎ – 2♦︎ = natural, inv.
 • Convenient for removing this hand type from   2wCB and 4th-suit-F1 auctions
 • Prevents 4th-hand from bidding ONE MAJOR.

Unpassed hand only

 • 2♥︎ = balanced 11-12
 • 2NT = 13-15 or 18+

2.2. We open ONE CLUB, they DOUBLE

We do not want to use 1NT as the contructive-raise-or-better because there’s a reasonable likelihood of wrong-siding a NT contract.

So in this case, we use:

 • 1NT = balanced 10–12
 • 1♠︎ = transfer to NT, typically with clubs

1♠︎ includes a constructive raise with five-clubs but opener typically assumes 3•3•3•4 with 7–9.

Since 1♠︎ is unlimited it’s fine to include stronger balanced hands, but probably not stronger hands with 5-card club-support because those are vulnerable to preemption. So:

 • 2♦︎ = LR+ of clubs

2.3. Opener’s rebid

Following a transfer to a major, opener accepts at the 1-level with 3-card support and anything less than a GF.

With 4-card support, opener raises as though responder had bid 1M.

Otherwise, opener makes normal rebid.

 • pass = three-cards in the doubled suit   ➤  responder can XX to play, no retransfer
