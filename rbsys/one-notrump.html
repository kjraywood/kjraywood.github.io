4.3. Vs double of Stayman

In order of priority:
 • 3♣︎ = 4-4 majors max
 • 2NT = 4-4 majors min
 • pass promises a stopper   (may have a stack)
 • reverse transfers (2♦︎ ➞ ♥︎, 2♥︎ ➞ ♠︎)   deny a stopper
 • Redble = double neg. (no stop, no M)
  ➤  scramble for a place to play,   Smolen is OFF

4.3.1. When opener passes

Responder can redouble as re‑Stayman, but must have at least two clubs and at least invitational values because opener might have a stack and be intending to pass.

With a garbage-Stayman hand (three-suiter short in clubs), responder bids 2♦︎ and opener corrects with a four-card major.

With short-clubs and a GF, responder bids 3♣︎ as re‑Stayman. Smolen is ON.

Other bids are as though opener had replied to Stayman with 2♦︎, although in this case, opener may still have a four-card major. It’s possible to miss a 4‑-4 major-suit fit when responder rebids 2NT and opener passes.

5.5. Vs double of transfer

 • redouble = no stopper

 • new-suit = shortness   ➤  opener assumes COG

A 3NT rebid is generally to play, but opener may continue with undisclosed extras following a previous-round min or pre-decline.

7. Interference over 1NT

Texas is ON when the interference is below  2NT.

When the interference is 2NT or higher, we treat the situation similarly to when they make a preemptive opening-bid in that strain.

7.1. Negative double thru 3S

  Countering Notrump Interference    –  Phillip Martin

 • take-out of bid suit   as though the interference was natural
 • creates force to 2♠︎   ➤  subsequent doubles are penalty

7.2. Vs penalty double

Transfers are on, redouble is one minor, 2♣︎ is both majors, and 2NT is both minors. Pass is to play but opener redoubles with a five-card suit, after which 2♣︎ is pass-or-correct and pass says, "I think you’ll make it."

With a 4-card major and 6-card minor (McCartney), responder is willing to compete to the 3-level. 2♠︎ is four spades and a six-card minor. 3m is four hearts and six in the bid minor.
