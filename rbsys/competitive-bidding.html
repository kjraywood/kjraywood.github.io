40.2. Scrambling 2NT

Used to find the best fit when partner forces you to bid at the three-level and we are unlikely to have game on power.

Applies when  • opps have bid to 2♥︎ or 2♠︎  • partner makes a take-out double  • RHO passes  • the auction is not defined as Lebensohl

It shows at least two places to play.

42. Doubling Artificial Bids

42.1. General Rules

Double of an artificial non-forcing bid is for takeout

It is take-out of the suit they bid, not the suit they show.

Examples  • artificial defence to our 1NT  • weak-only multi-2♦︎

If we cannot double a non-forcing bid with shortness in the bid suit, then we risk the auction ending there.

Double of an artificial forcing bid shows the suit

Examples  • Bergen Raises  • Drury Raises  • KC responses  • Stayman*  • Transfers*

* see next sections for exceptions

42.2. They open a weak-NT

If they open a weak-NT, double of their Stayman or transfer shows high-card strength (∼15+HCP). It is not lead-directing, or showing length in the suit bid.

42.3. They play transfers after their 1C opening

Double = take-out of the suit shown by the transfer.

Bidding the suit shown by the transfer is natural.

Their 1-level transfers only show four-card suits. When we have a length in the suit bid on our right, we may want to play in that suit so we take advantage of the extra step that their transfers have given us.
