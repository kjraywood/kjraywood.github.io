13.7. Fit-Showing Jumps & Splinters in comp.

By an unpassed hand, a jump to game in a new suit is to play; not fit-showing. Therefore the highest fit-showing jumps are four of a minor.

As per our 2oM NFB agreements, a jump to 3oM is natural and forcing; not fit-showing. Therefore, after we open a major and they overcall a minor, there is no fit-showing jump in the other major.

Fit-showing jumps are a single-jump when a non-jump in the same suit would be forcing.

If a double-jump in the unbid minor is below game, then it is a splinter.
